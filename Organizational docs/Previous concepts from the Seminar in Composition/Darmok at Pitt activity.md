## Activity: The "Darmok at Pitt" Lexicon Challenge

### üéØ Overview & Goal

In the *Star Trek* episode "Darmok," an alien race speaks a language that is incomprehensible to the "Universal Translator." The tool can translate the *words* (the "Performance"), but it fails to translate the *meaning* (the "Intent" or "Competence").

Why? Because their language is made entirely of metaphors based on shared cultural stories. "Darmok and Jalad at Tanagra" means "two people working together against a common foe."

Our Generative AI tools have the *exact same problem*. They are masters of "Performance" (plausible text) but have zero "Competence" (no shared context, no lived experience, no *stories*).

**Your Goal:** To create a "Pitt-Darmok" lexicon‚Äîa set of phrases that are perfectly understandable to a Pitt student but would be "AI-proof" and completely baffling to an LLM.

### üìù Your Task: Build the Lexicon

We will use a shared, collaborative document (like a Google Sheet) to build our lexicon. The document has three columns:

1.  **The "Darmok" Phrase:** The Pitt-based metaphorical phrase you invent.
2.  **The "Pitt" Meaning:** The actual, non-literal meaning or interpretation.
3.  **Example in Use:** A simple sentence showing how it's used.
